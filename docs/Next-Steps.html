Next Steps and Proposed Improvements

As we look to advance the application of our Rock-Paper-Scissors (RPS) algorithm towards the world of nocturnal seizure treatments, a comprehensive and strategic approach is important. 

The following proposal outlines key avenues for improvement, ensuring the algorithm's robustness, adaptability, and precision in monitoring and recognizing seizure patterns during patients' sleep.

Training Data

To strengthen the algorithm's foundation, prioritizing the collection of robust training data is the most important. This involves incorporating diverse scenarios, including variations in starting positions during the countdown phase. This modification aims to decrease susceptibility to different starting positions, enhancing the algorithm's versatility for diverse sleeping positions. By ensuring that we have a comprehensive dataset that captures various movements and positions, we can significantly improve the model's learning rates, equipping it to adapt to a wider array of user behaviors, especially during sleep.

Continous Data Collection

Implementing continuous data collection represents a step towards tailoring the algorithm for monitoring nocturnal seizures. This approach allows the armband to gather data over multiple nights, providing a better understanding of an individual's regular and irregular sleep movements. 

The extended monitoring period enhances personalization, ensuring that the algorithm becomes finely tuned to the specific patterns shown by the user. While the benefits are substantial, it's crucial to address the associated challenge of increased data storage requirements.

Precise Onset Detection

Refining onset detection capabilities remains critical for recognizing the swift initiation of seizures. Despite challenges faced in the previous iteration, the potential impact on prediction rates makes it a worthwhile avenue for future exploration. 

Enhanced onset detection would enable the algorithm to identify the duration of seizures promptly, a crucial factor given their typically brief duration. With the increased data volume from robust training and continuous collection, the significance of precise onset detection becomes even more pronounced.

Algorithm Adaptation

To tailor the RPS algorithm for its alternative application in nocturnal seizure treatments, the focus shifts towards identifying patterns indicative of seizures during sleep. Leveraging the improvements mentioned earlier, the armband could gain a substantial sample size of both regular and irregular sleep movements for training. Continuous data collection would facilitate personalized monitoring over several nights, offering a dynamic understanding of individual sleep patterns (which are very personal and complex!!).

Evaluating accuracy and feasibility

The testing accuracy of the Rock-Paper-Scissors (RPS) model, featuring all extracted features, yielded an accuracy of 95.97%. While this accuracy is generally high for a machine learning model, its translation into the context of a nocturnal seizure application highlights challenges and feasibility considerations. In the context of nocturnal seizure treatments, this level of accuracy poses potential risks due to its high error margin.

Misclassifications, even if infrequent, can have consequences for patient safety, emphasizing the need for a significantly lower error rate. The feasibility of adapting the RPS algorithm for nocturnal seizure treatments becomes complex when considering the sensitivity and speed required for seizure detection. The nature of timely intervention demands an exceptionally low margin of error, with a binomial distribution analysis suggesting that achieving a minimum accuracy of 98% is needed. 

There exists some challenges as the algorithm was initially trained for specific one-time movements, while nocturnal seizure movements can exhibit considerable variability even within the same category. Addressing this challenge will not easy, but not impossible. 

Improved controlled data and enhanced data collection methods have the potential to elevate the algorithm's accuracy by a substantial margin. These improvements are crucial for ensuring the algorithm's reliability and precision in this real-time application, where the stakes are high and the need for intervention is time-sensitive.
